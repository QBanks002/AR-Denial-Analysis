# 🏥 AR Claim Denial Revenue Analysis

Quantifying revenue lost to claim denials across a 200-claim medical accounts receivable dataset, with root cause breakdown, payer benchmarking, and recovery scenario modeling.

\---

## 📌 Business Question

> \*How much revenue is being lost due to claim denials, and what is the potential financial impact of reducing denial rates?\*

\---

## 📊 Dataset

Synthetic medical AR dataset — 200 claims across 7 insurance payers.  
All claims fall in the **181+ day aging bucket**, making recovery time-sensitive.


\---

## 🔍 Key Findings

|Metric|Value|
|-|-|
|Total AR balance|$136,623|
|Total denied balance|$31,625|
|Denial rate|24.5%|
|Industry benchmark|\~10%|
|Fixable denied claims|33 of 49|
|Highest denial payer|Cigna ($7,649)|

> The 24.5% denial rate is nearly \*\*2.5x the industry benchmark\*\* — representing significant recoverable revenue.

## 🗂️ Pipeline Overview

1. **Load** — read raw AR dataset, inspect shape, dtypes, null counts
2. **Clean** — parse dates, strip whitespace, tag denied and fixable claims
3. **Quantify** — calculate total denied balance, denial rate, % of AR at risk
4. **Root cause** — group denials by status code, separate fixable vs. non-fixable
5. **Payer analysis** — denial rate and denied balance per insurance payer
6. **Scenario modeling** — project revenue recovery at 25%, 50%, 75% reduction
7. **Worklist** — rank fixable claims by balance for immediate action
8. **Visualize** — denial by cause, payer, status, and recovery scenarios
9. **Export** — save all outputs to CSV for operational use
